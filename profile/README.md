# Operational Ecology

**Operational tools for conservation.**

[operationalecology.io](https://operationalecology.io)

---

Conservation research generates more imagery, audio, and field data every year than the people doing the work can process. Manual analysis stays the bottleneck — even when the questions are clear and the data already exists.

Operational Ecology builds production machine-learning tools that close that gap. We work with researchers, NGOs, and government agencies who already collect the data and already know what to look for — and we ship the systems that extract the signal at scale.

## Current Work

### scarwatch

Automated detection and longitudinal tracking of conspecific scarring events on killer whales, from standard photo-identification images.

Built on our peer-reviewed research on machine-learning detection of tooth-rake scarring patterns ([Barnhill et al., *Marine Mammal Science*, in press](https://operationalecology.io/research)), with a new generation of models targeting end-to-end event detection and population-scale longitudinal analysis.

In active development. Production access opening to research partners in 2026.

## Background

The team behind Operational Ecology has spent the last several years building applied ML systems for cetacean photo-identification — including [FIN-PRINT](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-02506-6) for individual killer-whale recognition and [finwave.io](https://finwave.io) for population-scale photo-ID workflows used by working biologists today. Operational Ecology extends that work into a focused product organization for conservation ML.

## Working with us

We partner with conservation research programs that have:

- An identified analytical bottleneck in existing imagery, audio, or video workflows
- A defined biological question that scale of analysis would help answer
- Data already collected, or a clear collection pipeline in place
